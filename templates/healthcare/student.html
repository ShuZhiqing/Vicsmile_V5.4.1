International students


Access to public dental care

If you are an international student you may be entitled to public dental care in Victoria.
Australia has agreements with several countries regarding public health service that allows international students in Australia to access public health service through Medicare. Find out if your country of origin has a mutual health agreement with Australia.

If you are eligible for Medicare you are entitled to public dental care in Victoria. Look up the hyperlinks for advantage and benefit you can claim from the government.

However, if you are not entitled for Medicare you cannot use public dental care services. Nevertheless, as required by law, you must have a private insurance coverage. Check with your OSHC provider whether you are covered for dental care or not.

Your local community dental clinic may sometimes cannot provide you the required service. In this situation, you may be given a voucher so that you can receive treatment from a private dentist that is valid for one to three months.

Call or visit the nearest dental clinic to you for question and appointment. If you do not speak English language very well, Translation and Interpreting Service may be useful for you.

Fee schedule

If you are entitled to public dental care in Victoria and aged 18 and over. The following fees are charged:
  • $28 fee per visit, to a maximum of $112 for a general course of care
  • $28 flat fee for an emergency course of care
  • $67.50 per denture, capped at $135 for full upper and lower dentures.
If you need any specialist services at Royal Dental Hospital Melbourne you have to pay fee no more than $338 for a course of care.

See this table below for fee summary:

Service Fee
General care $28 up to $112

Free of charge if you are
  • Under 18 years old
  • Having financial hardship
  • Registered with metal health and disability services
  • Serviced by undergraduate students.

Waiting list

You will generally be required to wait for several months if you do not fall as a person who has priority access.
You are entitled to priority denture care if you meet any of the following criteria:
  • They have no denture or natural teeth in one or both arches
  • They have missing front teeth, which causes aesthetic, functional or social problems that a denture could reduce
